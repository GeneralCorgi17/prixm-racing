# Ozzy — AI Tipster Engine v0.5

## What Is Ozzy?

Ozzy is an AI-powered horse racing tipster built on top of the Prixm scoring engine. He is not a replacement for the scoring model — he is a second opinion.

The Prixm engine scores every runner across 14 weighted factors (form, rating, trainer, jockey, going, class, draw, etc.) and surfaces the best picks of the day. Ozzy reads those picks and decides whether he agrees, disagrees, or has nothing to add.

Ozzy is powered by Claude (Anthropic's AI). He has a persistent memory, tracks his own record, reflects on his mistakes after results come in, and carries lessons from one day into the next. He speaks in first person and is deliberately opinionated — his job is not to validate the engine, it is to think independently.

---

## What Ozzy Does

### 1. Analyses Picks Pre-Race

When today's picks are loaded, Ozzy analyses the top-tier selections (NAP, WIN, STRONG). For each one he receives:

- The full factor score breakdown
- CDP analysis (Class, Distance, Going, Course — each rated Proven / Untested / Concern)
- Verification Pass results (a separate concern-scoring system that flags unverified positives)
- His own past record on similar picks (same venue, same going, similar conditions)
- His active convictions (pattern-based red flags he has learned over time)
- His last 5 BACKED calls and outcomes
- His last 5 OFF IT calls and outcomes
- Wrong calls he has documented with lessons attached
- Lessons he extracted from his own post-race reflections
- His own written performance reviews from previous days

He then outputs a single position word on the first line, followed by his honest reasoning.

### 2. Takes a Position

Every Ozzy analysis resolves to one of five positions:

| Position | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **BACKED** 🔥 | Ozzy endorses this pick. He may also disagree with the engine's suggested bet type. |
| **WITH IT** ✅ | Ozzy broadly agrees but has nothing specific to add. Often silent — no comment rendered. |
| **WATCHING** 🤔 | Interested but not committing. Worth monitoring. |
| **DOUBT** ⚠️ | Has concerns. Advises against backing. |
| **OFF IT** 🚫 | Actively opposes. Do not back this horse. If there's a better option in the same race, he names it. |

BACKED and OFF IT are the strong-signal positions. The others sit on a spectrum of confidence.

### 3. Self-Improves After Results

Every time race results are logged, Ozzy runs a full self-review chain automatically:

1. **Retro Audit** — fires his conviction patterns against historical picks to build up outcome data
2. **Reasoning Audit** — for each BACKED or OFF IT call, asks: was my reasoning sound given the pre-race data? Separate from whether the horse won.
3. **Stats Rebuild** — recomputes his full performance stats across all positions
4. **Day Reflection** — writes a 3–5 sentence post-race self-assessment comparing what he said before each race with what actually happened
5. **Lesson Extraction** — pulls 1–3 structured, actionable lessons from the reflection and stores them for future use

These lessons feed directly into every future API call, so Ozzy applies his own identified failure patterns to new picks.

### 4. Tracks a Conviction Library

Ozzy maintains a library of conviction patterns — specific situations where the Prixm score may be misleading. These start as candidates and must earn their place through real outcomes.

**Built-in convictions:**

| ID | Description |
|----|-------------|
| `going_unverified_heavy` | High going score but no verified run on heavy/soft ground in the last 12 months |
| `class_drop_masking` | Class drop inflating score — 3+ recent poor runs at higher class |
| `fresh_trainer_flat` | Trainer sends horse fresh after 90+ day break; trainer's fresh record is poor (<10% wins) |
| `phantom_cdp_course` | Course score present but based on a single run over 18 months ago |
| `competitive_prob_compressed` | Top-2 Bradley-Terry probability gap ≤5% — weak field inflating the edge |

A conviction becomes **active** after 3+ fires with a >60% strike rate. It expires if it fires 5+ times with a <40% strike rate. New convictions can be auto-generated by the reasoning audit when Ozzy identifies a new pattern.

## Shadow Mode

Until Ozzy has enough data to be reliable, he operates in shadow mode:

- He still analyses every pick and forms opinions
- He still saves all his reasoning to memory
- He does **not** render anything in the UI

Shadow mode exits when all three conditions are met:
- 80+ race results logged
- 5+ conviction outcomes recorded
- At least 1 active conviction

This prevents Ozzy from surfacing opinions before he has any meaningful track record.

---

## Memory

Ozzy's memory is stored in localStorage under the key `ozzyMemory`. It persists between sessions and contains:

- His conviction library with outcome tracking
- Per-position performance stats (BACKED, WITH IT, WATCHING, DOUBT, OFF IT)
- Stats broken down by venue and going
- Last 10 BACKED calls with outcomes
- Last 10 OFF IT calls with outcomes
- Notable wrong calls with documented lessons
- Up to 30 daily reflections (post-race self-assessments), sorted by date and archived after 45 days
- Up to 20 extracted lessons with condition tags (going, class, venue, race code)
- An audit log of all significant events (capped at 200 entries)

Each day's pick analyses are also cached separately (`ozzyDailyBriefs_YYYY-MM-DD`) so the same pick is never re-analysed within the same day.

## What Makes Ozzy Different From the Engine

The Prixm engine scores runners objectively against 14 factors. It cannot:

- Notice that a trainer's fresh record is historically poor
- Recognise that a horse's course score comes from a single run two years ago
- Know that Ozzy backed three similar profiles last week and all lost
- Apply a lesson from a Wednesday reflection to a Saturday pick
- Disagree with its own bet recommendation

Ozzy can do all of these. He is a layer of judgment above the numbers.

He is also fallible. He documents his wrong calls, rates his own reasoning quality independently of outcomes, and distinguishes "my read was wrong" from "the horse fell." The goal is not a tipster who is always right — it is a tipster who gets better.

## Technical Summary

- **Model:** Claude Sonnet 4.6 (all operations — analysis, reflection, lesson extraction, conviction mining)
- **API calls:** Only fire when verif concerns exist OR a conviction pattern fires. WITH IT never makes an API call — and even when it does, if the returned comment is under 20 words no panel renders.
- **Picks analysed:** NAP, WIN, STRONG only (not PLACE)
- **Files:** `engine/ozzy/ozzy_engine.js`, `ozzy_prompts.js`, `ozzy_ui.js`, `ozzy_audit.js`
- **Storage:** localStorage (`ozzyMemory` key) — results history loaded from file, not cached in localStorage
- **Status:** Live (out of shadow mode as of May 2026)

## v0.2 Changes (May 2026)

### Bug Fixes

**CORS / file:// loading** — HTML file previously relied on `fetch()` for `daily_race_data.json`. Browsers block `fetch()` from `file://` origin. Both racecard fetchers now also write `daily_race_data.js` (sets `window._raceDataFile`), loaded via `<script>` tag. HTML checks that global first, fetch second.

**localStorage quota crash** — `results_history.json` grew to 7.6MB. Caching it in localStorage exceeded the 5MB quota, crashing the `load()` function mid-execution and preventing the entire Ozzy self-review chain from firing. Fixed by evicting `resultsHistory` from localStorage on every load — it is always read fresh from `results_history.js` (script tag) or `results_history.json` (fetch).

**Day reflection not saving** — root cause was the 7MB `resultsHistory` key blocking all localStorage writes including `ozzyMemory`. Reflection would generate via API, get added to memory in-session, then fail to persist. Fixed by the quota eviction above.

**Day reflection wrong date** — fallback trigger in `load()` was using `window.D.date` (the race card date, which could be yesterday's data) instead of today's actual date. Fixed to always use `new Date().toISOString().slice(0,10)`.

**Reflection display ordering** — daily reflections were displayed in insertion order (newest generated, not newest date). After backfill, old dates appeared at top. Fixed by sorting by date descending before render.

### UI Improvements

**Stats display** — previous labels ("0/2 avoided", "called") were ambiguous. Redesigned to plain English:
- `BACKED / WITH IT / WATCHING`: `X won / Y called (Z%)`
- `DOUBT`: `X lost / Y doubted · Z won anyway`
- `OFF IT`: `X lost / Y opposed · Z won anyway`

Stats cards now use colour-coded bordered tiles in a 2-column grid instead of a flat row.

## v0.3 Changes (May 2026)

### UI Improvements

**BACKED bet disagreement label** — when Ozzy's recommended bet type differs from the engine's (`betRec`), the engine's suggestion is now shown as an explicit amber badge: "Engine says X ↑ Ozzy disagrees". Previously displayed as muted grey text, easily missed. Appears in both the pick panel and the Ozzy tab card.

### Safety

**`sanitiseMemoryForContext()`** — new function strips internal-only fields (`alerts`, `audit_log`, `shadow_mode`) from the memory object before it is serialised into the Claude API prompt. Streak/alert data was previously safe by omission (not used in context building), but is now safe by design. Reduces prompt token usage and prevents internal state leaking into Ozzy's reasoning.

## v0.4 Changes (May 2026)

### System Prompt Coverage

**System prompt wired into all API calls** — `OZZY_SYSTEM_PROMPT` was previously only present on the main pick analysis call (`ozzyAnalysePick`). Five API calls in `ozzy_audit.js` were missing it entirely, meaning day reflections, streak reflections, reasoning audits, lesson extraction, and conviction mining all ran against Claude's defaults rather than Ozzy's defined character.

All calls now include a `system:` argument:

| Function | System prompt used |
|---|---|
| `ozzyDayReflection()` | `OZZY_SYSTEM_PROMPT` — full character for voice output |
| `ozzyRunReasoningAudit()` | Minimal — preserves JSON output format |
| `ozzyExtractLessons()` | Minimal — preserves JSON array output |
| `ozzyMineConvictions()` | Minimal — preserves CANDIDATE_CONVICTION block format |

JSON-returning calls use a minimal system prompt rather than the full character prompt — applying `OZZY_SYSTEM_PROMPT` to structured calls would push the model toward free-text output and break parsing.

## v0.5 Changes (May 2026)

### Hard Silence Rule for WITH IT

**`parseOzzyResponse()` enforces silence at the parser level** — not just via prompt instruction. WITH IT responses with a comment under 20 words return `render: false`. The UI render gate in `renderOzzyPickPanel()` checks this flag and returns nothing — no panel, no placeholder text. The absence of a panel is the message.

This is a two-layer enforcement: the system prompt tells Ozzy to be silent when he has nothing to add; the parser enforces it regardless of what the model returns.

### BACKED Structured Parsing

BACKED responses require a structured second line: `BET: [WIN/EW/TOP N/SKIP] | CONFIDENCE: [HIGH/MODERATE/SPECULATIVE]`. The parser extracts `betRec` and `confidence` as discrete fields on the result object — not inferred from comment text.

When Ozzy's `betRec` differs from the engine's recommendation, an amber badge appears: "Engine says X · Ozzy disagrees". No badge when they agree.

### Lesson Relevance Filtering

**`buildRelevantLessons()`** filters Ozzy's lesson library before passing it into the API context. Each lesson carries condition tags (going, race code, class, venue). A lesson only reaches the API request if its conditions match the current race.

Legacy flat-string lessons (no tags) are treated as matching all conditions — no data loss, no regression.

Schema for tagged lessons:
```
{
  text: "one sentence — specific and actionable",
  conditions: { going, code, class, venue },  // null = matches all
  created: "YYYY-MM-DD",
  source_reflection: "reflection_YYYY-MM-DD"
}
```

Up to 5 most recent matching lessons are included per API call.

### ozzyStreakReflection() Removed

`ozzyStreakReflection()` fired an API call whenever Ozzy had 3 consecutive BACKED losses, telling him he was on a losing streak and asking him to reflect on it. This directly contradicted the existing design principle that streak data should not reach Ozzy's reasoning context — three consecutive losses in horse racing is variance, not a pattern, and making Ozzy softer during a bad run was the exact problem the design was built to avoid.

The function is removed entirely. The streak display in the stats UI is unchanged — it remains a signal for the user, not an input for Ozzy.

## v0.6 Changes (May 2026)

### Bug Fix: Reflection Chain Broken by Retro Audit Skip Guard

**Problem:** `ozzyRetroAudit()` has a skip guard — if `retro_audit_complete` is already in the audit log for a given date, it returns early. This is correct behaviour (no double-processing). But the early return path was not chaining to `ozzyDayReflection()`, breaking the full self-review chain.

**Scenario that triggered it:** User fetches results in two passes (e.g. partial results early, full results later). First pass runs `ozzyRetroAudit` and logs `retro_audit_complete`. Second pass triggers `ozzyRetroAudit` again — skip guard fires, returns 0, and `ozzyDayReflection` is never called. Reflection, lesson extraction, and conviction mining all silently fail.

**Fix:** Skip path in `ozzyRetroAudit()` now chains to `ozzyDayReflection()` with a 1s delay before returning:

```js
if(done){setTimeout(()=>{if(typeof ozzyDayReflection==='function') ozzyDayReflection(date);},1000);return 0;}
```

`ozzyDayReflection()` has its own skip guard (`reflections.find(r=>r.date===date)`), so this is safe to call multiple times — it only generates once per date. The full chain now always completes on results load regardless of how many times results are fetched.

### Bug Fix: Lesson Sort Using Wrong Field Name

**Problem:** `buildRelevantLessons()` sorted lessons by `b.date` but the lesson schema stores the date field as `b.created`. Every sort comparison returned `NaN` — lessons appeared in random insertion order, not recency order.

**Fix** (`ozzy_prompts.js`):
```js
// Before
.sort((a,b)=>new Date(b.date)-new Date(a.date))
.map(l=>`  [${l.date}] ${l.lesson}...`)

// After
.sort((a,b)=>new Date(b.created||b.date)-new Date(a.created||a.date))
.map(l=>`  [${l.created||l.date||'?'}] ${l.lesson||l.text}...`)
```
`||b.date` and `||l.text` fallbacks preserve backward compatibility with any legacy lessons using old field names.

### Bug Fix: Going/Venue Match Too Loose (Substring → Exact Normalised)

**Problem:** Going and venue conditions used substring matching:
```js
c.going.some(g=>rGoing.includes(g)||g.includes(rGoing))
```
`"good_to_soft".includes("soft") === true` — a lesson tagged for `soft` ground incorrectly fired on `good_to_soft` races. Same false-match risk for venue (e.g. `"york"` matching `"new_york"`).

**Fix** (`ozzy_prompts.js`): Added `norm` helper, both sides normalised before exact match:
```js
const norm=g=>g.toLowerCase().replace(/[\s-]+/g,'_');

const goingOk=!c.going||c.going.map(norm).includes(rGoing);
const venueOk=!c.venue||c.venue.some(v=>norm(v)===rVenue);
```
Also updated `rGoing` normalizer from `\s+` to `[\s-]+` to handle hyphenated going strings (e.g. `"good-to-soft"`).
